0. THE OPERATOR DIRECTIVE (Frank, 2026-06-26 — verbatim intent)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"Make the AAU section of astersports web the MAIN HUB for basketball — for the
PARENT. A family signs up, identifies their kids (my daughter is #5 on Legacy
Hoopers' LeagueApps Chris Ward team; my son is on the 8th-grade boys team), and the
hub pulls together ALL their kids' teams across every tournament we've uploaded.

It should:
  - Let a parent SELECT one or many teams to TRACK — picked from the inventory of
    teams the tournament scrape already discovered. That selection IS how they
    register their family to those teams.
  - NOTIFY them when a schedule is uploaded or changes, when a score updates, and
    when a game goes final.
  - Show the STANDINGS / rankings for the division their kid is in.
  - PREDICT the bracket: who's 1st/2nd, who advances to playoffs, where/when the
    bracket games are, AND an AI predictor of their ODDS of making the bracket
    based on that tournament's RULES (e.g. Zero Gravity: wins count only up to a
    20-pt margin; 2-way tie = head-to-head; 3-way tie = point differential).
  - Let parents UPLOAD their own film of a game; AI breaks it down into highlights
    filtered to THEIR child's jersey number (like the film feature we built).
  - Be FLEXIBLE but SIMPLE: pick 5th-grade-girls + 8th-grade-boys; or compare
    5th-grade-girls A vs B vs C divisions side-by-side.

This is a DIFFERENT VIEW from the app. The app is program-level (sign up, run a
program, comms). This is a family's single source of truth for all their kids'
teams — scheduling, rankings, standings, notifications, film. L99 collaboration."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PRODUCT IN ONE PARAGRAPH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A public, family-centric basketball hub. The organizing primitive is the FAMILY,
not the org or the team. A parent lands on the AAU hub, claims their kids by
pointing at the team(s) the scraper already discovered in a tournament, and gets a
live, push-notified dashboard: every kid's games, scores, division standings,
bracket-qualification math with AI odds, and their own uploaded film auto-clipped
to their kid's number. One account → every kid → every game → everywhere they play
(including across more than one program). This is the marketed selling point.

3. THE DECISIONS (each: REQUEST / EVIDENCE / OPTIONS+REQUIREMENTS / CC LEAN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

== D-FV1 : WHERE THE HUB LIVES (architectural fork) ==========================
REQUEST: Is the family hub (a) a section of astersports-web (.io AAU), (b) a new
  public "Family" view inside the aster-sports app, or (c) a standalone surface
  reading the same Supabase?
EVIDENCE: #125 placed "the hub" in the AAU/.io property. But ALL the data + 90% of
  the reusable UX (spine, EventCard, standings, push) lives in aster-sports. The
  app is already the system of record; astersports-web is a separate
  Manus/Drizzle/MySQL app (different stack) — it does NOT share the Supabase the
  scores/tournaments live in.
OPTIONS:
  (a) astersports-web .io section — marketing-aligned, but it would have to reach
      ACROSS to Supabase (new data layer in a MySQL app) — high friction, two
      sources of truth risk (Cat#30/AP#63).
  (b) New public family view in aster-sports — sits on the data + reuses the spine,
      EventCard, push, records math directly. Lives behind a public route
      (anon/publishable key, like the legacy-hoopers schedule page already does).
  (c) Standalone (new repo) reading Supabase — clean boundary, but re-implements
      the whole component vocabulary.
CC LEAN: (b) — build the family hub as a PUBLIC, family-scoped view in the
  aster-sports app, served by anon-safe SECDEF public RPCs (the get_public_*
  pattern we just shipped for legacy-hoopers). astersports-web .io links INTO it.
  The data and the reusable UX are both already there; (a) duplicates the data
  layer in the wrong stack. (Per AP#42: don't build a parallel system when the
  app already IS the system.)

== D-FV2 : THE FAMILY-CLAIM MODEL + CROSS-PROGRAM IDENTITY (load-bearing) =====
REQUEST: How does a family claim teams from the scraped inventory, and do we
  support a family whose kids span MORE THAN ONE program now or later?
EVIDENCE: 2.6 — identity plumbing is solid but SINGLE-ORG. The scraper's DISCOVER
  phase (#1102) already yields a per-division team inventory — the natural picker.
  But "claim a team my kid is on" has no model; today a parent is inferred from
  roster links inside one org.
OPTIONS:
  (1) Roster-tied only (MVP-safe): parent claims only teams where a roster link
      already exists (their real org). Simple, no new RLS surface, but breaks the
      "any tournament, any team, even cross-program" promise.
  (2) New family_subscriptions table: (family/guardian_id, team_id or
      tournament_team_id, claimed_at). A parent taps a scraped team -> a
      subscription row -> drives the hub feed. Org-agnostic key; RLS lets a
      guardian read public tournament data for any team they subscribed to.
      Requires: the table + RLS + a public "claim" RPC + a verification stance
      (open claim vs. prove-the-kid).
  (3) Full multi-org parent routing now: remove roleRows[0], add active-org /
      family-home, union parent_context_v across orgs. Biggest lift; unblocks the
      cross-program story fully but touches auth for every user.
CC LEAN: (2) as the spine, decoupled from org membership — the hub feed is driven
  by SUBSCRIPTIONS to scraped teams, NOT by org roster. This delivers the
  cross-program promise WITHOUT the full multi-org auth refactor (3), and isn't
  capped by org like (1). Open question for the architect: claim verification —
  open (anyone can track any public team, like following a sports team) vs.
  gated (prove the kid, jersey match). CC sub-lean: OPEN for tracking/standings/
  notifications (public data anyway); GATED only before anything family-private
  (film, a child's name on a card) is exposed. Frank to confirm the privacy line.

== D-FV3 : DIVISION / POOL / BRACKET SCHEMA (the missing standings substrate) =
REQUEST: How do we model division, pool, seed, and bracket so standings can be
  scoped to "this division in this tournament" and a bracket can be drawn?
EVIDENCE: 2.3 — standings are season-wide; 2.4 — bracket_label exists on games but
  there's no division/pool/seed/advancement schema. divisions table links to
  programs, not tournaments [VERIFY].
OPTIONS:
  (A) jsonb on tournaments (pools/divisions/bracket as a blob) — fast, but not
      queryable, weak RLS, repeats the dead-config trap (16.16).
  (B) Junction tables: tournament_divisions, division_pools (or pool_teams), and
      bracket_slots (round, seed, advancement rule). Queryable, RLS-safe,
      standings join cleanly. More migration work up front.
CC LEAN: (B) junction tables — this is the queryable spine the standings engine
  (D-FV4) and predictor (D-FV5) both stand on. Architect-scoped (DB schema +
  migration; CLAUDE.md 2.4) -> design-reviewed before code, owner applies. CC
  drafts the migration; does NOT apply without sign-off.

== D-FV4 : STANDINGS + TIEBREAKER ENGINE (rule-driven) =======================
REQUEST: Build a real standings engine that reads circuit_rules and applies the
  cap + tiebreakers, instead of the hardcoded winPct->point_diff in StandingsTable.
EVIDENCE: 2.1 circuit_rules already stores rules + point_differential_cap with
  Zero Gravity seeded; 2.3 nothing applies them. Tiebreakers Frank named: cap
  margins at 20; 2-way = head-to-head; 3-way = point differential.
OPTIONS:
  (i) Keep hardcoded, special-case Zero Gravity — fast, but every new circuit is a
      code change; can't go multi-tenant.
  (ii) Rule-driven engine: a pure function standings(games, rules) -> ranked rows
       with capped diff + head-to-head + N-way tiebreak, rules sourced from
       circuit_rules. Runs where D-FV1 lands (client-side pure fn first; promote
       to a SECDEF RPC if anon needs it server-side).
CC LEAN: (ii) rule-driven from day one — the rules table already exists, the cost
  delta is ~a day, and it's the only version that scales to other circuits/tenants
  (St Patrick next). Pure function, fixture-tested (AP#27 pure-with-injected-IO).

== D-FV5 : BRACKET-QUALIFICATION PREDICTOR + AI ODDS ========================
REQUEST: Given current standings + remaining games + the tournament's advancement
  rule (top-1/top-2 advance), show who's in, the clinch/elimination scenarios, and
  an AI "odds of making the bracket" number.
EVIDENCE: A ScenariosTab already exists (2.4) [VERIFY what it computes]. No bracket
  schema yet (D-FV3 supplies it). circuit_rules supplies the tiebreaker logic.
OPTIONS:
  (P) Deterministic scenario engine: enumerate remaining-game outcomes (small N in
      pool play), apply D-FV4 standings to each, count the fraction where the team
      qualifies -> a TRUE odds % + "you're in if you win" / "you need X to lose."
      Transparent, defensible, no hallucination.
  (Q) LLM predictor: feed standings + rules to a model, ask for odds + narrative.
      Fast to ship, but opaque and can be wrong on exactly the tiebreaker math
      that matters.
  (R) Hybrid: (P) computes the real number + clinch logic; an LLM writes the
      plain-English "what has to happen" around (P)'s output (never the math).
CC LEAN: (R) — deterministic math is the truth (pool-play outcome spaces are
  small enough to enumerate exactly), the LLM only narrates. This is the honest
  version of "AI predictor" and it won't embarrass us on a tiebreaker. Reuse
  whatever ScenariosTab already has as the starting point.

== D-FV6 : REAL-TIME NOTIFICATIONS TO PARENTS (push, not admin-draft) ========
REQUEST: Push to a subscribed family on (a) schedule uploaded/changed, (b) score
  update, (c) game final — without an admin in the loop.
EVIDENCE: 2.5 — push works (Stream A), triggers exist for schedule_changed +
  game_completed but they auto-DRAFT to Radar; there is no game_final/score_updated
  trigger and no push on the editorial path.
OPTIONS:
  - Add score_updated + game_final triggers on game_results; on the scraper's
    ingest, fan out send-push directly to the team's SUBSCRIBERS (D-FV2), bypassing
    the admin Radar for these public, factual events. Schedule-change push reuses
    the existing schedule_changed detection but routes to subscribers + push.
  - Opt-in model: a per-subscription notify preference (score / schedule / final),
    defaulting on for tracked teams.
CC LEAN: Build a SUBSCRIBER fan-out path for the three factual events, push-first,
  reusing send-push. Keep the admin-draft Radar for editorial comms (recaps with
  prose) — those two paths stay separate. Money/PII none here; not money-path. The
  trigger + a public-data push is routine once D-FV2/D-FV3 land.

== D-FV7 : PARENT-UPLOADED FILM + AI HIGHLIGHTS BY JERSEY (net-new track) =====
REQUEST: Parent uploads game film; AI clips highlights filtered to their child's
  jersey number.
EVIDENCE: 2.7 — entirely net-new: no storage bucket, no video schema, no AI, no
  per-jersey tagging. team_players.jersey_number gives the number to target.
OPTIONS:
  - Phase it LAST as its own track: (1) Supabase Storage bucket (private, family-
    scoped) + upload UI; (2) media schema (video -> tournament_game -> player);
    (3) an AI clip/jersey-detection service (Replicate or similar — the studio
    side already integrates Replicate, a reuse candidate); (4) per-child clip
    filter UI. Privacy: minors' video is the most sensitive surface on the
    platform — sub-processor + consent posture must be designed before any frame
    is uploaded.
CC LEAN: Carve film OFF the critical path as Track 2. It's the highest-effort,
  highest-privacy-risk, lowest-dependency piece — the standings/predictor/
  notification hub (Tracks 0-1) delivers the selling point without it, and film
  lands on top once the family-claim + identity model (D-FV2) is solid.

== D-FV8 : PUBLIC / ANON DATA EXPOSURE =====================================
REQUEST: The hub is public-facing. What's anon-readable vs. gated?
EVIDENCE: We already ship the get_public_team_schedule / get_public_team_records /
  org_is_public_listed SECDEF pattern (gated, PII-stripped). Standings + brackets
  are derived from the same published games.
CC LEAN: Standings, schedule, scores, bracket math, AI odds = PUBLIC via SECDEF
  RPCs (same gate as today). Family-private (a child's name pinned to a card,
  uploaded film, notification targeting) = behind the claim/subscription + auth.
  One clear line: public = the tournament's factual state; private = anything tied
  to a specific child's identity.

7. OPERATOR REFINEMENT (Frank, 2026-06-26, post-draft) — PAID PROFILE + CONTAINMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Verbatim intent: "Team yes — and in that team, under their PAID profile, they can
add their kids and upload their own highlights, so it's CONTAINED."

This refines the model in two load-bearing ways and adds one new decision:

7.1 THE HUB IS A PAID PRODUCT (new money-path surface).
  A parent holds a PAID family PROFILE scoped to a team. The paid profile is the
  container for that family's kids + their uploaded highlights. This is a
  SUBSCRIPTION -> Stripe -> MONEY PATH. Per CLAUDE.md 2.4 this is Architect-scoped
  AND a Frank confirm (the money path is Frank's to set). Stripe is now wired
  (live-mode keys + webhook set up this session), so the billing rail exists; what
  it gates (entitlement to the paid profile) is the new design.

7.2 "CONTAINED" RESOLVES THE PRIVACY LINE (answers Q1 + Q4).
  Kids and parent-uploaded highlights live INSIDE the paid family profile, scoped
  to the team — PRIVATE to that family, not public. This settles the boundary that
  was open in D-FV2/D-FV8:
    PUBLIC (anon SECDEF RPCs):  the tournament's factual state — schedule, scores,
       standings, bracket math, AI odds. No child identity.
    CONTAINED (paid profile + auth):  the family's kids, their roster pin to a
       team, their uploaded film/highlights, and notification targeting. Visible
       only inside that family's paid profile.
  So D-FV2's "claim verification" question is now answered by the PAYWALL: tracking
  public team data can stay open/free; ADDING KIDS + UPLOADING FILM happens only
  inside a paid, authenticated, team-scoped profile. The paid profile IS the gate.

7.3 CONTAINMENT SHAPE (structure implication).
  profile = (paying guardian) -> scoped to a TEAM -> holds that family's kids
  (added by the parent) -> holds highlights the parent uploads (per kid, by jersey).
  A family with kids on two teams holds two team-scoped slices under one paid
  account (or one profile spanning their teams — see D-FV9 below). Film never
  leaves the profile; D-FV7's storage bucket is private + profile-scoped, which
  also shrinks the minors'-video exposure surface (highlights are seen by the
  uploading family, not broadcast).

== D-FV9 : MONETIZATION / PAID-PROFILE ENTITLEMENT (NEW — money-path) =========
REQUEST: What does the paid profile entitle, and how is the subscription scoped —
  per team, per family, or per platform?
EVIDENCE: 7.1 — Stripe is wired this session (live keys + webhook). The app's
  money path (credits/Stripe) is Architect-scoped (CLAUDE.md 2.4) and Frank-set.
  No paid-profile entitlement model exists yet.
OPTIONS:
  (a) Per-team profile: a parent pays per team they add a kid to. Simple mental
      model ("pay for the team you follow"), but a 2-kid family pays twice.
  (b) Per-family account: one subscription unlocks adding ALL the family's kids +
      film across every team they're on. Cleaner family story; matches "one account
      -> every kid." Entitlement = the account, scope = all their claimed teams.
  (c) Freemium: public tracking + standings + odds are FREE (drives signups +
      marketing reach); ADDING KIDS + FILM + push targeting require the paid
      profile. Paywall sits exactly on the public/contained line from 7.2.
CC LEAN: (c) layered over (b) — free public hub for reach, ONE paid family account
  that unlocks kids + film + private notifications across all their teams. This
  matches the "single source of truth for all their kids" pitch and puts the
  paywall precisely on the containment boundary. Money-path: CC PREPARES the
  Stripe product/price + entitlement check; Frank SETS it; Architect reviews the
  entitlement/credit logic before any code merges (2.4 + the section-1 ledger
  invariants). CC does not wire billing logic without that sign-off.

7.4 OPEN QUESTIONS UPDATED:
  - Q1 (claim verification): ANSWERED by 7.2 — the paywall is the gate; public
    tracking free, kids+film behind the paid profile.
  - Q4 (film containment): ANSWERED by 7.1/7.3 — film is contained in the paid,
    team-scoped family profile; private bucket; not broadcast.
  - NEW Q6 (D-FV9): subscription scope — per-team (a), per-family (b), or freemium
    over per-family (c)? CC lean: (c)/(b). Frank to set price + tier; architect to
    review entitlement logic.
  - NEW Q7: does the paid profile REPLACE or COEXIST with the existing app's
    parent role? (The app already has free parent accounts inside an org. Is the
    paid hub profile a separate consumer product, or an upsell on the same
    identity? CC lean: same identity, paid hub is an entitlement ON TOP — avoids a
    second identity system, AP#42.)
