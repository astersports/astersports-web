================================================================================

  tournament → division → grade → gender → TEAM → (track) → assign to profile/kid (optional)

  In the data, a TM "division" already encodes grade+gender (e.g. "Boys - 5th").
  Render 02 surfaces that as TITLE "Girls · 5th grade" + a Girls/Boys toggle, teams
  listed with Pool + record.

01 DISCOVERY (public): paste/search a tournament → tap a tournament row → Screen 02.

02 TRACK ONE OR MANY (public browse, the multi-select that creates tracking):
   SEARCH-FIRST, filters assist (RATIFIED — supersedes render-literal gender→grade drill-down):
   • A single search box: "Search a team or club." Type "Legacy" → every Legacy Hoopers
     team across all divisions, each with grade/gender/pool/record. Zero drilling for the
     parent who knows their team (the people who pay).
   • Gender + Grade chips below it are OPTIONAL filters that NARROW, never gate. "Girl
     teams" = tap Girls; "just teams" = type the name. Neither forces the other.
   • Results grouped by CLUB; each club carries an inline "+ All <Club>" bulk-track
     (director/LeagueApps one-tap). Team rows = checkbox + name + "Pool A · 3–0" + a LIVE
     score badge if on the court now.
   • Sticky CTA: "+ Track N selected teams" → writes to the account's tracked set, then a
     skippable "assign to a kid?" nudge + "alert me on tip-off & final?" opt-in.
   • A team can be tracked with NO kid (fan/coach/director) — assignment is a SEPARATE axis.

   TEAM-AS-THE-UNIT (RATIFIED — the spine underneath everything):
   • You track the TEAM, not the tournament. Once tracked, the team FOLLOWS you across
     every tournament it enters — automatically, no re-adding. The unit a parent cares
     about is their kid's team, not a weekend's bracket. Set once, always know where/when.

03 MY TEAMS (account, live command center):
   • Tracked teams grouped BY PROGRAM (Legacy Hoopers · today · 9 games).
   • Live hero (Realtime score tick), glance stats (to-advance %, live now, today).
   • Scales 1 → 30 teams (director watching a whole club).

7c. THE PREDICTOR IS MATH (operator-clarified 2026-06-26 — "the predictor is really just math")
================================================================================
Frank: "What team beat what team by how much points; you're playing that team; that team is
gonna play another team — that's how you predict the odds." The predictor is transitive
margin math, transparent and exact — NOT a black box. Two layers, composed:

  (A) RESULTS → RATINGS. From every final game we have (who beat whom, by how many points).
      Each team gets a strength rating from its scoring margins, with the margin CAPPED per
      circuit (e.g. Zero Gravity +20, from circuit_rules) so a blowout can't over-inflate.
      Opponent-adjusted (a win over a strong team counts more) — a simple iterated power
      rating (Massey/Colley-style), recomputed as games resolve. Source: get_public_
      tournament_standings games[] + the circuit cap already in the bundle.

  (B) RATINGS → MATCHUP ODDS. A future/hypothetical game's odds = f(ratingA − ratingB):
      expected margin = ratingA − ratingB; win probability via a logistic on that gap. This
      is the head-to-head "odds to beat CT Hoops."

  COMPOSED into the two predictor surfaces:
   • Standings predictor (Screen 05, BUILT): "% to advance" = EXACT enumeration of remaining
     pool outcomes (already shipped) — answers "in how many remaining-game combinations do I
     advance." The margin-rating (B) adds the per-matchup win-probability the render shows
     ("In control · 82%") and the plain-English conditions ("Beat CT Hoops → clinched").
   • Road to the chip (feature 04): chain it down the bracket — you play B; B's slot feeds the
     winner of Pool C; project each slot by ratings; multiply the path to "odds to reach/win
     the final." Seeds fill in as real games resolve; every step traces to a real result.

  PROVENANCE (locked, per render): "Odds enumerated exactly · wording AI" and "Path exact from
  the bracket · seeds fill as games resolve." The math is exact and shown; only the sentence
  wording is AI. No fabricated probabilities — if inputs are missing, the surface says so.
