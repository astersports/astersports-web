0. PURPOSE & METHOD
=====================================================================
Frank walked the live AAU Hub on his phone (signed in as a parent tracking the
three "East Coast Storm" girls teams — Black / Pink / Blue) and sent 12
screenshots spanning the full flow: Browse/Find → Tournament Detail → Team
Detail → Home (tracked-teams dashboard). He asked for a comprehensive review of
"every single thing" — enhancements, bugs, visual issues — written up for the
architect.

This doc is that review. Every finding is grounded in BOTH the screenshot and
the actual component source (file:line). The goal frame Frank set is retention:
"make sure a parent who logs in gets hooked and stays forever." So findings are
judged not just for correctness but for whether they build or erode the habit.

2. WHAT'S WORKING — PRESERVE THESE (the hook already exists)
=====================================================================
P-1 [G] The UP-NEXT countdown hero (NextGame.tsx). Big gradient countdown
    ("2d 11h") → matchup → venue → game-time weather with an ACTIONABLE nudge
    ("bring water + shade") → one-tap Apple/Google directions → "Remind me to
    leave" that drops a real calendar event at leave-by (NextGame.tsx:163-173).
    Countdown + logistics + weather + calendar is precisely the bundle that
    makes a parent open the app the morning of a game. This is THE retention
    primitive; protect it. [UX]

P-2 [G] Honesty discipline is strong and should not be diluted. The codebase
    refuses to fabricate: BracketPath is type-level incapable of showing a
    probability (BracketPath.tsx:15, oddsPct?: never); StatusStrip blanks
    "to advance" until pool play wraps (StatusStrip.tsx:25-31); HeroSlot reframes
    a clinched team as "Still alive — pool play wrapped" instead of "no game"
    (HeroSlot.tsx:60-70); NextGame self-hides drive/weather until each real
    source resolves. This earns trust — keep it.

P-3 [G] The "you" highlight in division standings (blue row, "← you") and the
    decision-tier sort (live team floats to top and auto-expands,
    HubHomeV2.tsx:88-91) make the dashboard feel personal and alive.

P-4 [G] Smart-default expansion: one team → its card opens; many → only the
    most-urgent opens (HubHomeV2.tsx:83-87). Good for the multi-kid parent.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
A. PARENT-FACING COPY THAT SPEAKS ENGINEER (trust leaks)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
These violate the kindness/clarity mandate (aster-sports §16.3) and the
"no diagnostic copy a parent can't act on" rule (§16.14). None are bugs; all
erode the "this was built for me" feeling.

A-1 · P1 · routine — Provenance footnote under the countdown
  [G] NextGame.tsx:176-177 renders, under every up-next card:
      "Countdown + drive time from venue lat/lng. Weather via aster-weather."
  Two problems: (a) "venue lat/lng" and "via aster-weather" are internal
  plumbing a parent neither knows nor needs; (b) it PROMISES "drive time" that
  only renders when getOrigin() resolves (NextGame.tsx:128), so on many loads
  the caption advertises a feature the card isn't showing.
  → Recommend: delete the caption. The data is self-evident. If a provenance
    line is wanted, make it human and conditional (e.g. only when drive shows).

A-2 · P1 · routine — Browse empty/footer copy
  [G] BrowseTree.tsx:347: "Filters read structured columns — no name-parsing.
      Past seasons collapse; tap to browse for records."
  This is an internal engineering rule (the no-name-parsing doctrine) printed
  to the parent. Screenshot IMG_3786 shows it under a "Nothing in this filter"
  empty state (BrowseTree.tsx:276) when "Live & upcoming" returns 0.
  → Recommend: drop the doctrine line. Empty state reads simply, e.g.
    "No tournaments match these filters yet." + the existing "Clear filters".

A-3 · P1 · routine — "Outcomes enumerated exactly"
  [G] BracketPath.tsx:77 appends a green-checked line "Outcomes enumerated
      exactly" to the bracket-path card (IMG_3800).
  This is reassurance aimed at the architect/H1 honesty rule, not the parent —
  it reads like a system log. The honesty is real and worth keeping; the WORDING
  is the issue.
  → Recommend: remove the line from the parent surface, or fold confidence into
    the count copy itself (see A-4).

A-4 · P2 · routine — Raw scenario count is intimidating
  [G] BracketPath.tsx:62-65: "advances in 1081 of 4096 remaining scenarios"
      (IMG_3800). 4096 = 2^12 — an engineer's number. A parent reads "4096" as
      noise, not insight.
  → Recommend: lead with a human ratio, keep the exact count secondary, e.g.
    "Advances in about 1 in 4 ways the weekend can still play out (1,081 of
    4,096)." [UX]

A-5 · P3 · routine — Status-strip footnote jargon
  [G] StatusStrip.tsx:29-30: "To-advance computes when pool play wraps — no
      estimate before the model earns it." Honest, but "the model earns it" is
      insider phrasing.
  → Recommend: "We'll show who's advancing once pool play wraps." [UX]

B-1 · P2 · routine — Team name repeated up to 4× per card
  [G] On Team Detail (IMG_3792) within one viewport the team name appears as:

B-2 · P2 · routine — Long ALL-CAPS division names dominate & truncate

  → Recommend: a short label ("Girls HS · '26-27") as the primary, full string
    secondary/ontap. [UX] (Source of the long string is the ingested division

B-3 · P3 · routine — Live score "–" separator alignment

C. FLOW / INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE
---------------------------------------------------------------------
C-1 · P1 · architect-ish — The "ASTER-AGENT" console reads as a debug terminal
  [G] AgentConsole.tsx renders a monospace "terminal" with a green live-dot, a
      "watching" status, a narration line ("tracing 2 / 4 — next: Metro Hoopers"
      / "scanning 2 / 4 — no games live right now"), a progress bar that fills
      and RE-CYCLES every 2.6s (AgentConsole.tsx:49-55), and a chip row
      (Record/NEXT/Path/Scores) that LOOKS tappable but is decorative
      (role="img", aria-hidden children). Seen on Team Detail (IMG_3792) and
      Tournament Detail (IMG_3788), sitting ABOVE the real up-next hook.
  [UX] To a parent this is the most confusing element in the app: a progress bar
      that never completes reads as a stuck loader; "tracing 2/4" is jargon; the
      fake tabs invite taps that do nothing; and it pushes the genuine countdown
      below the fold. It is a deliberate brand device (the "live agent" language
      lifted from the landing FrontierSection) and HubHomeV2 already excludes it
      on purpose (H8, HubHomeV2.tsx:22) — which is the tell that it doesn't
      belong on a working parent surface either.
  → Recommend (architect call): demote it BELOW the countdown on the detail
    pages, or drop it from authenticated parent surfaces entirely and keep it on
    the marketing/landing page where "watch the agent work" earns attention. CC
    lean: drop on parent surfaces; the countdown is the hero, the console dilutes
    it. [UX]

C-2 · P1 · E · architect — Home surfaces only ONE game; no "your weekend" agenda
  [G] HubHomeV2 selects a SINGLE most-urgent hero (mostUrgent(ranked),
      HubHomeV2.tsx:73-74). A parent with three teams playing the same weekend
      sees one countdown; the other two games are reachable only by expanding
      each accordion card. The status strip counts ("today: N") but doesn't list.
  [UX] The highest-value daily view for a multi-kid parent is "here is your whole
      weekend in order": Sat 9:00 Pink @ House of Sports, Sat 1:30 Black @ ...,
      Sun 10:00 Blue @ ... — one agenda, one screen. That is a stronger
      open-every-day hook than a single hero.
  → Recommend: an aggregate "Next up across your teams" agenda (chronological,
    all tracked teams, next 48-72h) as Zone 1 or directly under the hero. Reads
    over data the hub already fetches (getTrackedTeamSchedule). Architect-scoped
    only because it's a new IA surface, not because of data. [UX]

C-3 · P2 · routine — Directions footnote vs. the buttons it describes
  See A-1; also a flow point — the caption sits between the maps row and the
  "Remind me to leave" button, adding a line of noise in the primary action zone.

E. CORRECTNESS / LANGUAGE BUGS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
E-1 · P1 · routine — Weather string can self-contradict
  [G] NextGame.tsx:134-138 renders "{temp}°F · {description} — {advice}". When a
      severe/rain WARNING coexists with a clear-sky DESCRIPTION it reads
      "103°F · clear sky — severe weather — check before you go" (IMG_3795). The
      condition descriptor and the warning are independent fields, so "clear sky
      — severe weather" looks like a contradiction.
  → Recommend: when w.isSevereWarning || w.isRainWarning, suppress the plain
    description and lead with the warning (e.g. "103°F · severe weather — check
    before you go"). weatherAdvice() already centralizes the advice string
    (NextGame.tsx:21-27); the fix is at the render concat (line 137).

E-2 · P2 · routine — Waze button (DECISION ALREADY MADE: remove)
  [G] NextGame.tsx:155-158 renders a third "Waze" directions button. The main
      aster-sports app removed Waze entirely (operator-directed 2026-06-13;
      Waze name/address search mis-routed on Frank's device even with verified
      coords). Frank confirmed in this session: remove it here too for
      consistency.
  → Action: drop the Waze <a> (NextGame.tsx:155-158), leave Apple + Google;
    update the "Apple / Google / Waze" comment at NextGame.tsx:144. Verify
    buildDirections.waze has no other consumer before removing the field. [V]

E-3 · P3 · routine — "to advance" counter semantics
  [G] StatusStrip "to advance" shows the count of CLINCHED tracked teams
      (HubHomeV2.tsx:61-77 → clinchedCount). The label "to advance" could read
      as "teams that still need to advance" rather than "teams that have clinched
      a bracket spot."
  → Recommend: label it "clinched" (matches the value), or "in the bracket". [UX]

G. ENHANCEMENTS — "STAYS FOREVER" (retention strategy)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The hub today is entirely TOURNAMENT-BOUND. When a tracked team's event ends,
there is no reason to return until the next one is ingested. To convert a
tournament utility into a daily habit:

G-1 · E · architect — Weekend agenda (see C-2). The single biggest daily-open
  lever for the multi-kid parent.

G-2 · E · architect — Proactive notifications. Today retention is pull-only
  ("Remind me to leave" is the one push, and it's manual). The deep hooks:
  (a) "Your game just went final — 49-53" score push;
  (b) "Next tournament posted — bracket releases Wednesday";
  (c) "Bracket game set: Sat 10:00 vs Metro Hoopers."
  Backend/eventing work — architect-scoped; worth its own decision doc.

G-3 · E · architect — Evergreen between-tournaments content. The Film tab exists
  (bottom nav) but the season has gaps. Candidates: a per-player / per-team
  season recap, season-over-season records, a "summer in review" card. Gives the
  app a heartbeat when no game is on the board (the HeroSlot already has a
  graceful "Season complete" rest state — HeroSlot.tsx:64-70 — that could host a
  recap CTA instead of a dead end).

G-4 · E · routine — Make the rest states pull forward. HeroSlot's "Season
  complete / Still alive — pool play wrapped" states (HeroSlot.tsx:60-81) are
  honest but terminal. Add a forward action ("Browse next tournament", "See the
  full bracket") so an idle visit still has somewhere to go.

G-5 · E · routine — Share affordance. A parent's instinct after a win is to text
  family. A one-tap "share this matchup / result" on the up-next and recent cards
  turns parents into a free acquisition channel. [UX]
