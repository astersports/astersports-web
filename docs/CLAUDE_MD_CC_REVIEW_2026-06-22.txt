C. CONTRADICTION TO RESOLVE (rule vs. shipped code) — Architect ruling needed
----------------------------------------------------------------------
- C1. §5 says: "Never add an env-coupled assertion to the unit suite (asserting
  `process.env.*` values) — it red-lines CI for every lane; assert posture in a
  boot check or the `/api/studio/posture` endpoint instead."
  But the shipped dark-posture guard does exactly that: server/cronSecret.test.ts:22
  and :26 assert `process.env.STUDIO_SCALE_LIVE` / `STUDIO_DENSITY_LIVE`
  `.not.toBe("true")`. [GROUNDED]
  These are the deliberate Flip-Authority §3.5 dark-default guards.
  Nuance that matters: the rule's stated rationale is "red-lines CI for every lane,"
  but the `.not.toBe("true")` pattern PASSES when the var is unset (clean CI) — so it
  does NOT red-line. The blanket prohibition therefore forbids, on its face, a test
  that is actually CI-safe AND is the very posture guard §3 depends on.
  CC lean: scope §5's rule to "don't assert a SPECIFIC value (`toBe(x)`) of
  process.env" (that pattern fails when unset), and EXEMPT the dark-default
  `.not.toBe("true")` guard — or relocate that guard to a boot/posture check per the
  rule's own pointer. As written, the rule and the shipped guard contradict.
  Bearing: open PR #76 (create-org) adds a STUDIO_CREATE_ORG_LIVE `.not.toBe("true")`
  guard in the same file, following the existing pattern — its disposition rides on
  this ruling.
